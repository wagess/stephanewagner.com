contexte: "APES Québec, an association dedicated to supporting people with disabilities, wanted to modernize its aging website to better reflect its values of inclusion and accessibility. The previous site no longer met current standards in design, accessibility, and performance."
probleme: "An outdated, low-engagement website with a confusing content structure and complex navigation"
hypothese: "A full redesign focused on accessibility, modern design, and a clear information architecture would improve visitor engagement, strengthen the association's credibility, and increase online visibility."
solution: "==Act as a catalyst for the internal team:== Inclusive design and ==content restructuring==, SEO optimization. Development of a custom site with a CMS that's easy for administrators to use."
methodologie:
  - Technical and ergonomic audit of the existing site
  - Co-creation workshops with the governance team
  - Prototyping and wireframe mockups
  - Iterative development with regular reviews
  - Administrator training for the new site
livrables:
  - Site architecture collaboration
  - Content analysis and mapping
  - Participatory workshop facilitation
  - Content strategy collaboration
resultat:
  - 20% increase in website traffic
  - Improved conversion rate (service requests, registrations), ==+ 2,000 connected pharmacists==
  - Better search engine visibility (top 3 for several keywords)
  - Positive user feedback on accessibility and ease of use
challenges:
  - Managing and integrating the complexity and diversity of the association's member types into the platform
  - Evangelizing the team on new design practices
